# Bright-TV-Analysis.
### Project overview:
This project focuses on analyzing Bright TV viewership data to understand user behavior and uncover key consumption patterns. With the growing importance of data-driven decision-making in the media and entertainment industry, this analysis aims to provide insights into how audiences interact with content across different dimensions such as content type, time of consumption, demographics, and geographic location.

The findings from this study are intended to support strategic decisions in content planning, customer value management (CVM), and targeted marketing initiatives.

### The Aim:
This project aims to analyze Bright TV data in order to understand what content users consume, when they consume it, who the core audience is, and where the engagement is concentrated, ultimately identifying the key drivers of viewership and engagement.

### Objectives:
- To identify the most-watched content and channels driving overall engagement.
- To analyze viewing patterns over time, including time-of-day and weekday vs weekend trends.
- To determine the core audience segments based on age group and gender.
- To evaluate the geographic distribution of viewership across provinces.
- To measure engagement intensity by comparing total sessions with unique users.
- To detect and account for data quality issues, such as missing demographic or location data.
- To generate actionable insights that can inform business and marketing strategies.

### Data Preparation and Processing:
The dataset used in this project required significant cleaning and transformation before analysis. SQL queries were written and executed in Databricks to merge multiple tables, standardize fields, and prepare the data for analysis.
During this process, several intermediate steps involved renaming columns and restructuring tables to improve data consistency and usability. As a result, the SQL scripts may contain multiple transformations and naming adjustments.

**It is important to note that:**
The final query in the SQL files represents the completed and cleaned dataset

### Key insight:
- Channels such as SuperSport and ICC Cricket generated the highest number of sessions, indicating strong audience interest in live and event-based programming.
- Additionally, some channels showed significantly higher session counts compared to unique users, suggesting high repeat engagement. 
- This indicates that users return multiple times to consume the same content, likely due to scheduled programming or ongoing events. 
- Viewership patterns vary significantly by time of day and day of the week.
- The core audience is primarily composed of adult and mature viewers, with significantly higher engagement compared to younger or senior segments.
- Gender analysis shows a strong male-dominated audience, suggesting that current content offerings may appeal more to male viewers. 
- Viewership is highly concentrated in certain regions, particularly Gauteng.
